The Personal Statement (PS) is the essay that is also called the Personal History Statement, the Diversity Statement, etc. Not every school asks for it, and if they do I’ve heard that it’s particularly used for fellowship determination. I have found this essay sooo tricky because it requires a fine line between personal reflection outside of your career that reveals more about why you will be an excellent researcher in your career.

To be honest, I don’t think that I truly knocked the PS out of the park. [Here's](https://www.reddit.com/r/gradadmissions/comments/nl0boo/diversity_statements_101_an_essay_guide_for/) a really great reddit article that I found after the fact which addresses a lot of different fears about the PS very well. 

After reflecting a lot my GRFP application, I think my “Broader Impacts” section (which then turned into my PS in PhD applications) was the weakest part of my application. I will share why in case it helps others to not make the same mistake: I was so worried about the fact that I didn’t have a big, overarching community service-based physics activity that I had been involved with in undergrad that I ended up throwing in way too many smaller ideas into my PS. I talked about presenting at conferences, doing outreach, training new students in the lab, being the SPS room manager (I had only been for a few months by then), learning about global perspectives on climate change while doing research in Japan, teaching a piano student throughout undergrad as a Musical Empowerment volunteer, and literally even more. To avoid making the same mistake I did, I would suggest aiming for one or a few main ideas and explain why those ideas are important to you, then relate them to who you would be in grad school.
